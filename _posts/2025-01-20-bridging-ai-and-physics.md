Welcome to my research log! 🧪💻

As a Ph.D. candidate at Chung-Ang University, my daily routine involves oscillating between two very different worlds: **Physics-based simulations** (like Molecular Dynamics & FEP) and **Data-driven Artificial Intelligence** (like GNNs & Generative Models).

### The "Accuracy vs. Speed" Dilemma

In traditional Computer-Aided Drug Design (CADD), we often face a trade-off:
* **Physics (e.g., FEP):** Extremely accurate and reliable, but computationally expensive and slow.
* **AI (e.g., Deep Learning):** Lightning-fast processing of millions of compounds, but sometimes lacks physical interpretability ("Black Box").

### My Approach: The Hybrid Model

My recent work, including **MetaboGNN** and **FEP-based Lead Optimization**, aims to bridge this gap. I believe the future of drug discovery lies not in choosing one over the other, but in **integrating them**.

* Using **AI** to rapidly screen and propose novel scaffolds.
* Using **Physics (FEP)** to rigorously validate and optimize those candidates with high precision.

In this blog, I will share my journey of building these pipelines, solving technical errors (yes, the dreaded CUDA errors!), and occasional insights from my life as a beetle breeder. 🪲

Stay tuned!
